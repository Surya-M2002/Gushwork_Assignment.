Asked Questions

HDPE pipes offer excellent corrosion resistance, leak-proof joints through heat fusion, high flexibility, and a long service life of over 50 years. They are lightweight, easy to install, and maintain water purity by preventing scale buildup and bacterial growth.

HDPE pipes are designed for a service life of 50 to 100 years when installed according to industry standards. Their durability against environmental stress, chemicals, and UV radiation makes them a long-lasting solution for infrastructure projects.

HDPE pipes are primarily joined using heat fusion techniques like butt fusion, electrofusion, or socket fusion, which create a leak-proof, monolithic joint stronger than the pipe itself. Mechanical fittings can also be used for certain applications or transitions.

HDPE pipes are widely used in municipal water supply, sewage and drainage systems, industrial chemical transport, irrigation, mining, and as conduits for electrical and telecommunication cables due to their versatility and strength.

Yes, HDPE pipes are the preferred choice for natural gas distribution. Their flexibility allows for easy installation in various terrains, and their leak-proof fusion joints provide the high level of safety required for gas transport systems.
